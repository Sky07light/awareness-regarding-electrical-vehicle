Benefits of Electric Vehicles

Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs)

Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) run entirely on electricity stored in rechargeable batteries. Benefits of BEVs include:

  • Zero tailpipe emissions
  • Reduced dependence on fossil fuels
  • Lower fuel and maintenance costs
  • Quiet and smooth driving experience
  • Promotion of renewable energy integration

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs)

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) have both a conventional internal combustion engine and a battery-powered electric motor. Benefits of PHEVs include:

  • Ability to drive on electricity or gasoline/diesel
  • Lower fuel consumption and emissions compared to conventional vehicles
  • Extended driving range with gasoline/diesel backup
  • Federal and state incentives for purchasing

Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs)

Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs) combine an internal combustion engine with an electric motor. Benefits of HEVs include:

  • Improved fuel efficiency compared to traditional vehicles
  • Reduced emissions and environmental impact
  • Regenerative braking to recharge the battery
  • No need for charging infrastructure
